Before we jumped into the code portion of these posts I wanted to ensure that everyone was on a similar page regarding A*.

So… what is it?

“In computer science, A* (pronounced “A star”) is a computer algorithm that is widely used in pathfinding and graph traversal, the process of plotting an efficiently traversable path between points, called nodes. Noted for its performance and accuracy, it enjoys widespread use. However, in practical travel-routing systems, it is generally outperformed by algorithms which can pre-process the graph to attain better performance.” — compliments Wikipedia

Yeah yeah, that’s the technical definition – which is still nice, but what does it mean?

Essentially the A* is this:

“A computer program that’s used to find an efficient solution to the question ‘How do I get from point A to point B?’” — compliments me

What A* is:

  1. Efficient – it’s fast and performant.
  2. Accurate – it will provide a great solution to the question.

What A* is not:

  1. Perfect – the answer may not be the perfect solution to the question, but it will be an accurate estimate.
  2. Searching over a known area.

In the above quote from Wikipedia, it notes that A is generally outperformed by algorithms that “pre-process the graph”, meaning that the “world” or “space” that you’re searching through to find a path from A to B is unknown in A.

In other words – the algorithm will start at point A and start searching through the area to find what “spaces” are available to move to, and will continue to do so in the direction of point B – searching for the shortest path to point B.

The Challenge

As this school season is coming to a close – we’ve been encouraged to work on problems that are more difficult than hard. Problems that won’t help us build a website or create the next big widget – but problems that are a genuine challenge to solve.

To support us in that challenge our lead instructor, Jeff Casimir, posted up some data structures and algorithms problems for us to take a shot at.

Here’s where the videogames come in.

Among the listings of challenges was one called The A* Search Algorithm.

It’s algorithms such as A* that made possible my many many hours spent in my college dorm playing Starcraft.

But what does it do? In a really small nutshell – it can be used to calculate the path your character should travel when you’re at point A and you want to go to point B – and there’s junk in the way.

The Goal

I want to document my process of reproducing this algorithm with Ruby in a series of blog posts.

My solutions likely won’t be perfect, but I’m not concerned about perfection right now – I just want to make the thing work. Once that’s delivered, we can evaluate and refactor. But you can’t do the latter without the former.

Why Ruby? – Because that’s what I’ve been learning for the past four and a half months at a place called gSchool, and I like it.
